Who We Are

Marung Sebaka Technologies is a South African innovation company with a long-term vision for inclusive growth. We are building across sectors where access, participation and dignity matter most — creating technology-led pathways that can support people who are often underserved by traditional systems.

Our public presence is intentionally simple. We speak about purpose, direction and impact because trust begins with clarity, while deeper operational detail belongs in the right private and professional settings.

"Technology should not leave people behind. That belief is not a mission statement — it is the founding design principle of everything we build."

— Madichaba Milosevic · Founder & Director

Our Name

Marung is a Sesotho word meaning clouds. Sebaka means place. Marung Sebaka — a place in the clouds. It speaks to accessibility, reach and the aspiration that the systems shaping everyday life should be reachable by everyone, from anywhere, on any device.

Our Commitment

As a South African company, we are committed to building with context and responsibility. We understand the communities we serve — not from a distance, but from lived experience. That context shapes every decision we make, from product design to partnership selection to how we engage with the world.

What We Stand For

Principles, not slogans.

◈
Inclusion by design

◉

Where We Work

Our focus.

We build across three sectors where the gap between people and systems is wide — and where practical, inclusive technology can meaningfully change that.

Three Sectors

Where access matters most.

01Fintech

We believe financial technology can widen participation by making everyday financial access more practical, more connected and more inclusive. The goal is not complexity for its own sake — it is meaningful access that helps people stay active in the economy. Across South Africa and the SADC region, millions of people remain on the edge of formal financial systems despite contributing to the economy every day. Inclusive fintech changes that — not by building more products for those who already have access, but by designing specifically for those who do not.

02Healthtech

We believe health technology can support stronger communities when access to information, services and digital pathways becomes easier and more equitable. Inclusion in health is part of inclusion in society. Digital health solutions designed around accessibility — on any device, in any language, at any literacy level — can extend the reach of health systems into communities that traditional infrastructure cannot serve efficiently. The opportunity is significant and the need is real.

03Agritech

We believe agricultural technology can strengthen resilience, opportunity and local economic activity by improving access, visibility and participation across food and farming ecosystems. Smallholder farmers, cooperatives and rural communities are often the least connected to the digital tools that could improve their yields, their market access and their economic stability. Agritech designed for their realities — not for large commercial operations — addresses a genuine and largely unmet need across the African continent.

Why Inclusion Matters

Inclusion is a design principle.

Not a slogan. Not a metric. A principle that shapes how we think about people, communities, services and long-term socio-economic change.

26M+

South Africans who are unbanked or underbanked — excluded not by poverty, but by architecture and access barriers in existing systems.

60%+

Of underserved recipients in South Africa have no smartphone — meaning digital solutions that require apps or data exclude the majority by default.

SADC

A region of 350+ million people where cross-border economic participation is essential to daily life for millions of families and communities.

SDG 10

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 10 — reduced inequalities — including a specific target to reduce remittance costs for cross-border transfers to below 3% by 2030.

Across South Africa and the broader region, too many people remain on the edge of formal systems — financial, health and agricultural — even while contributing to the economy every day.

Inclusive technology can help reduce barriers, improve convenience, widen access and support more equal participation in the ecosystems that shape everyday life and long-term opportunity.

For Marung Sebaka Technologies, inclusion is not a positioning choice. It is a founding principle — embedded in how we think about design, how we evaluate solutions, how we measure impact and how we build partnerships.

The communities we build for are not edge cases or secondary markets. They are the primary design consideration in everything we do.

Inclusive innovation, in practice

Inclusive innovation means designing for the person with the basic phone, not just the person with the latest smartphone. It means thinking about the woman in the informal economy, the smallholder farmer, the domestic worker sending money home — and building backward from their realities, not forward from assumptions. That is the Marung Sebaka Technologies approach. That is the only approach that creates durable, meaningful change.
