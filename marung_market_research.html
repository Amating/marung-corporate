01 · FNB RESEARCH

The FNB eWallet — R43 Billion Forced to Cash

FNB's own data confirms the scale of the problem — and exactly why Marung exists. In 2025, FNB processed 69 million eWallet transactions worth R43 billion. The average transaction was R600. Every single recipient was forced to withdraw cash at an ATM, Shoprite, Pick n Pay, or fuel garage.

Source — FNB, March 2026: "In 2025, over 69 million transactions worth R43 billion were processed through eWallet, with FNB customers sending, on average, R600 per transaction to recipients who can then withdraw the funds as cash without a bank account."
FNB has now evolved its offering into a digital wallet via WhatsApp — because they saw the same problem Marung identified. But FNB's solution requires data, a smartphone, WhatsApp, facial recognition, and consent to First Rand marketing. And the money still exits as cash. FNB validated the market. Marung solves it.
