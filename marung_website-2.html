About Marung Cloud
Built from a lived frustration

Marung was not built from a market research report. It was built from a moment — receiving money digitally and being forced immediately back into cash.

In April 2025, Madichaba Milosevic received money via FNB eWallet. It arrived digitally — on her phone, in seconds. And then the system did what it always does: it forced her to withdraw cash.

That became a question: why does digital money still terminate in cash for millions of Africans?

The answer was not technology. It was architecture. Nobody had built the layer between digital money arriving and people being able to live digitally afterward.

Marung was conceived that month. By May 2026, the company was incorporated, CMS RM Partners engaged, a Sponsor Bank conversation active, and a working proof-of-concept deployed. Twelve months from insight to institution-ready.

"In 1997, I was part of the team that launched YFm in Johannesburg. We did not create the youth market — we found it, built for it, and plugged into infrastructure that already existed. Marung is the same pattern. The market exists. The behaviour exists. We are simply the product the market has been waiting for."
Madichaba Milosevic · Founder
