Regulatory & Compliance Architecture

Built to be licensed, not tolerated.

Marung operates within South Africa's established regulatory framework from day one. The compliance architecture is not an afterthought — it is the foundation the product is built on.

Primary Legislation
National Payment System Act 78/1998

Marung operates as a registered Third-Party Payment Provider (TPPP) under the NPS Act. Registration pathway secured through a sponsor bank — not requiring independent SARB licensing.

Regulator
South African Reserve Bank

Full SARB compliance architecture built into product design. Payments Ecosystem Modernisation Programme is a direct regulatory tailwind for Marung's operating model.

Sponsor Bank Model
BIN Sponsorship & Virtual Card Issuance

Payment infrastructure partner provides BIN sponsorship, virtual Mastercard issuance, KYC infrastructure, and SARB compliance cover. 35-nation SADC footprint included.

Identity & KYC
FICA Compliance

Know Your Customer protocols embedded in the onboarding flow via infrastructure partner. Risk-tiered wallet limits in line with Financial Intelligence Centre Act requirements.

Data Protection
POPIA Compliance

Protection of Personal Information Act compliance built into data architecture. User data sovereignty maintained within South African cloud infrastructure.

Cross-Border
SADC Corridor Compliance

Cross-border transfers structured to comply with exchange control regulations and destination-country central bank requirements across 6 initial corridor markets.

Cloud Infrastructure

South African data residency. By design.

All Marung infrastructure is hosted within South African data centre regions — ensuring data sovereignty, POPIA compliance, and sub-10ms latency for USSD interactions. Cloud infrastructure partner selected with Financial Services industry competency and South African operations. AWS af-south-1 (Cape Town) and Azure South Africa North provide the primary and secondary regions.
